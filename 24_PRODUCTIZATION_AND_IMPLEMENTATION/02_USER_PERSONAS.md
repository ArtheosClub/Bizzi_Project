# 1. Purpose

This document defines the primary and secondary user personas for Bizzi Platform MVP.

It answers the product question:

```text
Who are we building Bizzi for first?
```

---

# 2. Persona Strategy

Bizzi must not start by serving every possible company role.

The MVP focuses on users who directly feel operational chaos and have authority to introduce a new operating model.

The first target user must satisfy three criteria:

```text
High pain
High authority
High willingness to experiment
```

---

# 3. Primary Persona

## P01 — The Overloaded Business Owner

### Profile

```text
Founder, owner or managing director of a small or medium business.
```

Typical company size:

```text
5–100 people
```

Typical industries:

- services;
- logistics;
- trade;
- consulting;
- construction;
- agencies;
- professional services;
- small manufacturing;
- ecommerce operations.

---

### Situation

The owner is still the central operating system of the company.

Most important coordination happens through:

- chats;
- calls;
- email;
- personal memory;
- spreadsheets;
- informal instructions;
- repeated explanations.

---

### Core Pain

```text
Everything depends on me.
```

The owner feels that:

- tasks get lost;
- responsibilities are unclear;
- employees wait for instructions;
- decisions are repeated;
- knowledge stays in people’s heads;
- managers do not see the whole picture;
- growth increases chaos;
- AI tools do not understand the company structure.

---

### Desired Outcome

The owner wants to say:

```text
I can finally see my business as a system.
```

Desired results:

- fewer lost tasks;
- clearer responsibility;
- better delegation;
- visible operating map;
- less personal bottleneck;
- reusable company knowledge;
- AI support inside company structure.

---

### Why This Persona Buys

This persona buys when Bizzi shows:

- immediate clarity;
- visible gaps;
- recommended next actions;
- operating dashboard;
- reduced dependence on personal control.

---

### First-Hour Value

Within the first hour, this user must receive:

```text
Initial Business Operating Map
```

The map must show:

- key functions;
- missing owners;
- first recommended agents;
- priority processes;
- urgent operating gaps.

---

### MVP Features Needed

- company workspace;
- onboarding intake;
- business operating map;
- function registry;
- responsibility assignment;
- task routing;
- dashboard;
- decision log;
- memory capture.

---

# 4. Secondary Persona

## P02 — The Operations Manager

### Profile

```text
Person responsible for daily execution and coordination.
```

Possible titles:

- Operations Manager;
- Chief of Staff;
- COO;
- Office Manager;
- General Manager;
- Project Coordinator.

---

### Situation

This person coordinates people, tasks, deadlines and processes across the business.

They often work between the owner and the team.

---

### Core Pain

```text
I am constantly chasing people, clarifying responsibilities and restoring context.
```

Pain points:

- unclear task ownership;
- informal processes;
- repeated follow-ups;
- missing status visibility;
- scattered information;
- weak accountability;
- no single operating view.

---

### Desired Outcome

The operations manager wants:

- one view of open work;
- clear owners;
- reusable processes;
- fewer manual follow-ups;
- visible execution rhythm;
- better reporting to the owner.

---

### Why This Persona Uses Bizzi

This persona uses Bizzi because it makes operational coordination visible and structured.

---

### MVP Features Needed

- task routing;
- process registry;
- operating dashboard;
- responsibility map;
- decision log;
- audit trail;
- process memory.

---

# 5. Secondary Persona

## P03 — The Business Analyst / Consultant

### Profile

```text
External or internal specialist helping the company structure its operations.
```

Possible titles:

- Business Analyst;
- Management Consultant;
- Process Consultant;
- Transformation Consultant;
- AI Consultant;
- Operational Advisor.

---

### Situation

This persona helps companies map processes, identify gaps and improve management systems.

---

### Core Pain

```text
Every client company is chaotic in a different way, and documenting the operating model takes too long.
```

Pain points:

- interviews take time;
- knowledge is scattered;
- process maps are static;
- recommendations are hard to operationalize;
- clients do not maintain documentation;
- AI outputs are not connected to operating structure.

---

### Desired Outcome

This persona wants Bizzi to help generate:

- operating map;
- function registry;
- gap analysis;
- responsibility map;
- process drafts;
- improvement backlog;
- client-ready reports.

---

### Why This Persona Uses Bizzi

Bizzi becomes a consulting accelerator and operating model builder.

---

### MVP Features Needed

- guided onboarding;
- operating map generation;
- function registry;
- process drafts;
- exports;
- audit trail;
- improvement recommendations.

---

# 6. Future Persona

## P04 — The Department Owner

### Profile

```text
Leader of a specific business function.
```

Examples:

- Head of Sales;
- Head of Marketing;
- Finance Manager;
- HR Manager;
- Logistics Manager;
- Customer Support Lead.

---

### Core Pain

```text
My function has tasks, people and processes, but no clean operating system.
```

---

### Future Value

Department owners will use Bizzi to manage their function, agents, processes, decisions and memory.

This persona is important, but not the first MVP buyer.

---

# 7. Future Persona

## P05 — The AI Agent Operator

### Profile

```text
User responsible for configuring and supervising AI agents inside the company.
```

---

### Core Pain

```text
AI tools are powerful but disconnected from authority, process and responsibility.
```

---

### Future Value

This persona will manage:

- agent roles;
- agent permissions;
- agent escalation rules;
- agent performance;
- tool access;
- memory access;
- audit logs.

This persona becomes more important after MVP.

---

# 8. Anti-Personas

Bizzi MVP is not primarily built for:

- large enterprise procurement teams;
- IT departments looking only for infrastructure tools;
- users who only want a generic chatbot;
- companies looking only for CRM;
- companies looking only for accounting software;
- users unwilling to describe their business structure;
- organizations expecting full automation without governance.

---

# 9. Persona Priority

MVP priority order:

```text
P01 — Overloaded Business Owner
P02 — Operations Manager
P03 — Business Analyst / Consultant
P04 — Department Owner
P05 — AI Agent Operator
```

Primary product decisions must optimize for P01 first.

---

# 10. Buying Trigger

The strongest buying triggers are:

- growth creates chaos;
- owner becomes bottleneck;
- tasks are repeatedly lost;
- responsibilities are unclear;
- new employees require too much explanation;
- company wants to use AI but lacks structure;
- owner wants visibility without micromanagement.

---

# 11. Activation Trigger

The user becomes activated when Bizzi produces a useful first operating map.

Activation moment:

```text
The user sees the company structure, gaps and next actions more clearly than before registration.
```

---

# 12. Retention Trigger

The user returns when Bizzi becomes the place where work is organized.

Retention drivers:

- open tasks;
- decision history;
- recurring processes;
- memory entries;
- dashboard visibility;
- agent recommendations;
- owner status review.

---

# 13. Product Implications

Because P01 is the primary persona, the MVP must prioritize:

- simple onboarding;
- immediate clarity;
- plain language;
- operating map;
- visible gaps;
- owner dashboard;
- suggested next actions;
- low setup burden.

The MVP must avoid:

- complex configuration;
- enterprise-heavy terminology;
- long setup projects;
- technical-first onboarding;
- feature overload.

---

# 14. Final Persona Statement

```text
Bizzi MVP is built first for the overloaded business owner who wants to transform company chaos into a visible, structured and AI-supported operating system.
```

This persona defines the first product experience, MVP priorities and activation criteria.

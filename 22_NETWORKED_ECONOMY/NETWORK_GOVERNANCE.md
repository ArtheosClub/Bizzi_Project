# 1. Purpose

Network Governance defines the constitutional framework for governing interconnected markets, platforms, ecosystems, enterprises, AI agents, and economic participants operating within the Networked Economy.

---

# 2. Mission

Establish a trusted governance model that enables autonomous participation, coordinated decision-making, transparent accountability, regulatory compliance, and sustainable value creation across distributed economic networks.

---

# 3. Architectural Position

```text

↓
```

---

# 4. Core Principle

Distributed economic systems require shared governance to preserve trust, interoperability, resilience, and fairness.

---

# 9. Decision Governance

Defines decision rights, approval workflows, delegation rules, escalation paths and accountability across network participants.

---

# 10. Participation Governance

Defines admission criteria, participant obligations, certification, suspension, exit policies and trust requirements.

---

# 11. Data Governance

Defines ownership, classification, sharing policies, retention, privacy, lineage and cross-network interoperability.

---

# 12. AI Governance Integration

Ensures AI systems comply with enterprise AI policies, explainability, safety, human oversight and auditability.

---

# 13. Compliance Integration

Supports legal, contractual, regulatory and industry-specific compliance across jurisdictions.

---

# 14. Governance Metrics

Measures policy adoption, governance effectiveness, compliance rates, decision quality, trust indicators and audit readiness.

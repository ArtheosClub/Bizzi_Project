# 1. Purpose

Network Risk Management defines the framework for identifying, assessing, monitoring, mitigating and governing risks that emerge across interconnected markets, platforms, enterprises, ecosystems, value networks and AI-driven economic interactions.

---

# 2. Mission

Protect the stability, resilience and continuity of the Networked Economy by proactively managing systemic and participant-specific risks while enabling sustainable growth and trusted collaboration.

---

# 4. Core Principle

In a networked economy, unmanaged local risks can propagate into systemic failures.

# 9. Systemic Risk Management

Continuously evaluates cascading failures, cross-network dependencies, contagion effects, market instability and critical infrastructure exposure.

---

# 10. Early Warning

Uses observability, intelligence, anomaly detection, predictive analytics and AI recommendations to identify emerging threats.

---

# 11. Mitigation Strategies

Includes redundancy, diversification, isolation, contingency planning, automated controls, governance interventions and coordinated response.

# 12. Governance Integration

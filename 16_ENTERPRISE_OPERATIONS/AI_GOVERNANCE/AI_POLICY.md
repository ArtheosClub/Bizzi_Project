**Status:** Canonical AI Governance Specification  
**Architecture Layer:** 16_ENTERPRISE_OPERATIONS  
**Governance Domain:** AI_GOVERNANCE  
**Owner:** AG001_CEO  
**Operational Owner:** AG002_Chief_Orchestrator  
**Risk Owner:** AG005_Risk_Manager  
**Audit Owner:** AG003_AI_Auditor

---

# 1. Purpose

AI Policy defines the mandatory rules, constraints, permissions, responsibilities, and governance requirements for the use of AI within the Art of Business enterprise.

---

# 2. Mission

Ensure that all AI capabilities operate safely, lawfully, ethically, transparently, and in alignment with enterprise objectives.

---

# 3. Policy Scope

This policy applies to:

- AI agents;
- language models;
- prompts;
- workflows;
- automated decisions;
- external AI services;
- AI-generated content;
- AI-assisted business processes.

---

# 4. Core Principle

AI may assist execution, but accountability remains human-owned.

---

# 5. Policy Objectives

- accountability;
- transparency;
- explainability;
- security;
- compliance;
- auditability;
- human oversight.

---

# 6. Permitted Uses

AI may be used for:

- analysis;
- recommendations;
- workflow automation;
- reporting;
- drafting;
- operational assistance.

---

# 7. Restricted Uses

Restricted uses require explicit approval.

Examples:

- financial commitments;
- policy overrides;
- legal interpretations;
- customer-impacting autonomous actions.

---

# 8. Prohibited Uses

AI must not:

- bypass governance controls;
- operate without ownership;
- access unauthorized data;
- execute outside assigned authority.

---

# 9. Human Oversight Requirements

Human approval is mandatory for:

- strategic decisions;
- material financial decisions;
- compliance exceptions;
- high-risk AI actions.

---

# 10. AI Ownership Requirements

Every AI capability must have:

- business owner;
- operational owner;
- risk owner;
- audit owner.

---

# 11. Data Usage Policy

AI systems must:

- respect data classification;
- minimize data exposure;
- log access;
- follow retention policies.

---

# 12. Prompt Policy

Prompts must be:

- versioned;
- reviewed;
- auditable;
- attributable.

---

# 13. Agent Authority Policy

Agents may act only within delegated authority boundaries.

---

# 14. Decision Policy

AI-assisted decisions require:

- rationale;
- confidence indication;
- audit trail;
- owner attribution.

---

# 15. Security Policy

AI systems must enforce:

- authentication;
- authorization;
- audit logging;
- least privilege access.

---

# 16. Risk Policy

AI risks must be:

- identified;
- assessed;
- monitored;
- mitigated;
- reviewed.

---

# 17. Compliance Policy

AI activities must align with enterprise compliance obligations and governance controls.

---

# 18. Incident Policy

AI incidents must be:

```text
Detected
↓
Escalated
↓
Investigated
↓
Remediated
↓
Reviewed
```

---

# 19. Audit Requirements

All material AI actions must be auditable.

---

# 20. Policy Violations

Violations trigger:

- investigation;
- escalation;
- remediation;
- governance review.

---

# 21. Policy Review Cadence

```text
Quarterly Review
Annual Policy Reapproval
```

---

# 22. KPIs

- AI Policy Compliance Rate
- Policy Violation Rate
- Human Oversight Coverage
- AI Audit Coverage

---

# 23. Governance Ownership

AG001_CEO owns AI policy accountability.

AG002_Chief_Orchestrator owns policy execution.

AG005_Risk_Manager owns policy risk oversight.

AG003_AI_Auditor owns policy audit assurance.

---

# 24. Architectural Role

AI Policy is the foundational control layer of AI Governance.

```text
AI Capability
↓
AI Policy
↓
Controlled AI Behavior
↓
Trusted Enterprise Outcome
```

It establishes the mandatory rules governing all AI activity within Art of Business.

# 1. Purpose

AI Economic Agents defines how autonomous and semi-autonomous AI agents participate in global economic systems to sense signals, support decisions, coordinate workflows, execute authorized actions, optimize value flows, and assist markets, platforms, ecosystems, enterprises, and institutions.

# 2. Mission

Enable safe, governed, auditable, risk-aware, and human-aligned AI economic participation across the Global Intelligent Economy while preserving accountability, transparency, resilience, and trust.

- unique agent identity;
- owner identity;
- operating context;
- jurisdiction context;
- authority credentials;
- lifecycle status;
- audit traceability.

---

# 10. Authority Model

Authority levels:

```text
Level 0 — Observe Only
Level 1 — Recommend
Level 2 — Route for Approval
Level 3 — Execute Pre-Approved Actions
Level 4 — Execute Conditional Actions
Level 5 — Restricted Strategic Authority
```

Level 5 requires explicit executive approval and continuous audit.

---

# 11. Human Oversight Model

Human oversight is required for:

- irreversible economic actions;
- high-risk market actions;
- cross-border compliance exceptions;
- major financial commitments;
- participant suspension or removal;
- strategic market entry or exit;
- policy-impacting decisions.

## 1. Purpose

The Enterprise Capability Map defines what the Art of Business enterprise must be able to do in order to operate as an AI-orchestrated company.

Capabilities are stable business abilities that connect:

```text
Vision
↓
Strategy
↓
Capabilities
↓
Processes
↓
Functions
↓
Agents
↓
Tools
↓
Actions
↓
Results
```

The Capability Map is the bridge between strategic intent and executable enterprise design.

---

## 2. Capability Principles

### P01. Capabilities Before Agents

Agents exist to operate capabilities. Capabilities do not exist because agents exist.

### P02. Stable Business Abilities

Capabilities should remain relatively stable even when tools, agents, workflows, or vendors change.

### P03. Every Capability Has Ownership

Every capability must have a responsible enterprise owner.

### P04. Capability-to-Function Traceability

Every capability must map to enterprise functions and responsible agents.

### P05. Capability Maturity

Every capability can be assessed, improved, automated, and governed.

## 19. Capability Maturity Levels

### Level 1 — Defined

Capability exists and is documented.

### Level 2 — Owned

Capability has an accountable owner.

### Level 3 — Operated

Capability is supported by processes and agents.

### Level 4 — Measured

Capability has KPIs and performance tracking.

### Level 5 — Optimized

Capability is continuously improved.

### Level 6 — AI-Orchestrated

Capability is operated through agents, memory, context, reasoning, and execution systems.

- automation potential;

- data availability;
